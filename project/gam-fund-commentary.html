CI GAM Fund Commentary

Case Study

Modernizing the fund commentary page for GAM. In addition to redesigning the page and making it appear more modern, I also designed the accompanying AEM component.

I decided to utilize a tile format rather than the aged list version that was currently on the site. This would make it easier to navigate and give it a more modern spin.

Filters like “quarter” and “year” were also removed based on lack of use from the users and to reduce redundancy — the list is now compressed and no longer expanded — therefore, certain filters were no longer necessary. This meant a cleaner look and easier style o navigation.

“Fund commentary” has also been removed from the commentary title since that’s also redundant, i.e. CI Canadian REIT ETF – Fund Commentary – June 2024

#3E71AA #203469 #000

Having a default 4x4 display seemed like the best way to go considering the sum total of commentaries that are usually housed on the page. It meant less scrolling while also not overwhelming the user with too much information.

Designing the AEM component

In addition to the page design, I also tackled the AEM component. While the page design accounted for the end user, the AEM component accounted for the content designers. The middle men who would input the data onto the page as intended. For this component I worked closely with the dev team and QA’d the component.

The component made the update process a lot more seamless and significantly cut down the time needed to update the page.

  • The fund title is displayed on the tile, i.e. if the fund title is “CI Enhanced Government Bond ETF – June 2024” and Marret Asset Management is selected under “Team” then this is how the tile will be displayed:
  • Team should have the 6 different options
  • Accent colour refers to the colour of the download icon and the colour of the team name. The default should be #3e71aa which is being used on the current fund commentary page. There should be three other choices: sapphire, black, white.
  • The colour choices for tile background colour should be the same as the background colours on the container component: none, black, white, sapphire, slate, gold, link, graphite, platinum, grey, burgundy, cobalt. “white” should be the default colour.
  • Tile outline refers to the drop shadow. There should be two options: drop shadow and none. “Drop shadow” should be the default.
  • The default fund title style should be the same paragraph styling that’s currently being used on the fund commentary page. The other font styles should be available as a choice, i.e. Headline 1 – Dark, Headline 1 – Light, Headline 2 – Dark, Headline 2 – Light, etc.
